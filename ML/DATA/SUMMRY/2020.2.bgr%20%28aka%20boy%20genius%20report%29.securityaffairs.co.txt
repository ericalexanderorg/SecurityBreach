Hackers share SQL databases from unsecured AWS buckets, including the archive belonging to the BGR tech news site in India. Hackers are sharing SQL databases from unsecured Amazon S3 buckets, one of them belongs to the BGR tech news site in India. The data leak was first reported by experts from the security firm Under the Breach, the full SQL backup contains , emails, hashed passwords, and other information. The site has about two million monthly users, it has over 210,000 followers on Twitter. "A"full SQL dump" refers to all the posts on the site along with access credentials for authors and administrators. According to the hackers that shared the links to the archive, the overall dump contains at least 36,000 emails and logins for the affected websites. Experts found 16 SQL dumps contained in a 7 ZIP archive.
