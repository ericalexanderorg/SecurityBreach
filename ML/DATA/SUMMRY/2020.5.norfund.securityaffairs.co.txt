Norway's state investment fund, Norfund, suffered a business email compromise attack, hackers stole $10 million. Hackers stole $10 million from Norway's state investment fund, Norfund, in a business email compromise attack. Norfund is a private equity company established by the Norwegian Storting in 1997 and owned by the Norwegian Ministry of Foreign Affairs. The fund receives its investment capital from the state budget. The fraudsters compromised the Norfund email system and monitored communications between the employees of the fund and their partners for months. The attackers created a Norfund email address to impersonate an individual authorized to transfer large sums of money through the bank Norfund. "Through an advance data breach, the defrauders were able to access information concerning a loan of USD 10 million from Norfund to a microfinance institution in Cambodia." reads a notice published by Norfund.
