The data contained "Personally identifiable information" of students at the university. In a statement there was "No evidence" malicious activity had caused the data breach. UTAS said the data that was breached "Is used to inform the ways the university supports students in their studies". The identifiable data was sent to all users with a utas. The university spent this morning contacting all of the affected 19,900 students. Vice-Chancellor Professor Rufus Black said the university had responded quickly to secure the information. The university has notified the privacy regulator, the Australian Information Commissioner.
